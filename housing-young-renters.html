The proof: young renters face far higher cost burdens

"Unaffordable" = a household spends more than 30% of pre-tax income on shelter. In the 2021 Census, households that had recently moved into a rental were far more likely to be cost-burdened than established renters — and the gap is widest for the youngest adults.

Takeaway: The affordability squeeze concentrates at the moment of entry. Young people signing their first leases pay today's market rents with the least savings to absorb them — 18–24 recent renters are cost-burdened at nearly double the rate of established renters their age. Policy aimed at "renters" broadly can miss this: the sharpest pressure point is the transition into the market.
