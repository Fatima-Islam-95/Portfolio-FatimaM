Professional Summary

Modelling complex systems for sustainable decisions

I'm a Graduate Research Assistant at the University of New Brunswick, where I build and study mathematical models of how populations and ecosystems change over time. Most of my work uses MATLAB and R to run simulations, test stability, and apply optimal control theory, all aimed at finding sustainable ways to manage fishery systems.

What I enjoy most is taking a messy real-world problem and turning it into something a model can actually answer. My work sits across dynamical systems, quantitative modelling, and ecological impact assessment, and I care about solutions that are grounded in data and genuinely sustainable.

Core Research Focus

Ecological modelling & sustainable yield

Most of my research looks at how fish populations behave across connected habitats, and what that means for harvesting them sustainably. I combine simulation, stability theory, and optimal control to weigh economic yield against the health of the ecosystem.

1

Population Dynamics

Develop and analyze complex mathematical models of population dynamics, focusing on dispersal effects on biomass in multi-patch fishery systems.

2

Quantitative Analysis

Use MATLAB and R to simulate model behaviour, perform stability analysis, and identify equilibrium points that predict long-term ecological outcomes.

3

Optimal Control Theory

Apply control theory to resource-management models, investigating strategies to achieve maximum sustainable profit in fishery systems.

4

Predictive Modeling

Build predictive models for multi-species population dynamics, a quantitative framework for resource assessment and process development.

5

Optimization Analysis

Leverage optimization to determine efficient resource-management strategies, balancing profit or yield with ecological sustainability (biomass).

6

Sustainable Yield Modeling

Model Maximum Sustainable Yield (MSY) to set data-driven quotas and support sustainable harvesting processes.

7

System Translation

Translate complex real-world systems into formal mathematical models to enhance evidence-based decision-making and process improvement.

Research Projects

Mathematical modelling in practice

Population Dynamics in a Chemostat: A Two-Species Modeling Approach

Two-species ODE model · April 2024

A system of non-linear ODEs (with Monod growth kinetics) for two microbial species competing for a single substrate in a continuous-flow bioreactor. Analyzes how dilution rate, substrate availability, and species interaction drive stability, coexistence, and competitive exclusion.

Read full paper (PDF) →

Profit Analysis due to Migration Asymmetry in a Two-Patch Fishery Model

Bioeconomic optimal control · April 2025

A spatially explicit bioeconomic model with logistic growth, constant harvesting effort, and temperature-dependent asymmetric migration γ(T). Derives conditions for a profitable equilibrium and the critical fishing effort, showing that moderate migration asymmetry can substantially raise maximum economic yield (MEY).

Read full paper (PDF) →

Patch Model of Grass Distribution in Salt Marshes

Spatial ODE model · December 2024 · with O. Grimard

A first-order non-linear ODE patch model for two salt-marsh grasses (Spartina alterniflora and S. patens) across a discretized stress gradient, incorporating intra- and inter-specific competition and species-specific stress resistance. Establishes equilibria and examines zonation and stability.
